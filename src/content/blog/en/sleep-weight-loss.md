## The Science of Sleep and Weight

### What Happens When You Sleep Poorly

When you sleep less than 7 hours, your body triggers a cascade of hormonal effects that directly sabotage your weight loss:

**1. Ghrelin Spikes (+15%)**
- This is the hunger hormone
- It pushes you to eat more
- Especially high-calorie foods

**2. Leptin Drops (-20%)**
- This is the satiety hormone
- You never feel "full"
- You eat beyond your needs

**3. Cortisol Rises**
- This is the stress hormone
- It promotes abdominal fat storage
- It increases sugar cravings

**4. Insulin Becomes Less Effective**
- Increased insulin resistance
- Sugar is more easily stored as fat

### The Numbers That Make You Think

| Sleep Duration | Impact on Weight Loss |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Less than 5h | +55% obesity risk |
| 5-6h | +23% obesity risk |
| 7-8h | Reference risk |
| More than 9h | +21% obesity risk |

**The Key Study**: Researchers followed two groups in identical calorie deficit.
- Group A: 8.5h of sleep ‚Üí -3.1 kg of fat
- Group B: 5.5h of sleep ‚Üí -1.4 kg of fat (55% less!)

Same diet, radically different results.

## Why You Eat More When Tired

### It's Biological, Not Personal Failure

When you're tired, your brain pushes you toward caloric foods. It's not lack of willpower ‚Äî it's survival.

**The mechanism:**
1. Lack of sleep = lack of energy
2. Brain seeks quick energy
3. Sugary/fatty foods are most "efficient"
4. Reward system activates (+24% activity)
5. You give in to cravings

**Average result**: +385 calories consumed per day of poor sleep.

## The 7 Pillars of Weight-Loss Sleep

### 1. Regularity (Most Important)

**The problem**: Irregular bedtime and wake times
**The solution**: Same time ¬±30min, even on weekends

### 2. Environment

**Temperature**: 60-65¬∞F (16-18¬∞C) ideally
**Darkness**: Total (mask if needed)
**Silence**: Earplugs or white noise
**Screens**: Out of the bedroom

### 3. Evening Nutrition

**Avoid after 6pm:**
- Caffeine (coffee, tea, cola, chocolate)
- Alcohol (disrupts deep sleep)
- Heavy meals
- Quick sugars

**Prioritize:**
- Light dinner 3h before bed
- Tryptophan-rich foods (turkey, banana, nuts)
- Chamomile or valerian tea

### 4. Physical Activity

**Yes but**: No intense exercise 3h before bed
**Ideal**: Exercise in morning or early afternoon

### 5. Pre-Sleep Routine

30-60 minutes before bed:
1. Dim the lights
2. Turn off screens
3. Relaxing activity (reading, warm bath)
4. Next day prep (reduces anxiety)

### 6. Stress Management

Stress is sleep's #1 enemy:
- Guided meditation (5-10 min)
- 4-7-8 breathing
- Gratitude journal

### 7. Strategic Napping

**If needed**: 20 minutes max, before 3pm
