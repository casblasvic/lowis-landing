You downloaded a free calorie counting app. You started with motivation. Two weeks later, you gave up.

**It's not your fault.** The problem is the very model of these applications.

## The Problem with "Free"

Let's start with a simple economic truth: **if it's free, you're the product**.

Free apps make money in two ways:
1. **Advertising**: Ads between every action
2. **Your data**: Sold to third parties

Neither of these options incentivizes them to actually help you lose weight. On the contrary: the longer you stay on the app without results, the more money they make.

### The Real Cost of "Free"

| What you think | The reality |
|----------------|-------------|
| It's free | You pay with your time |
| It does the job | It does the minimum |
| I'm saving money | You're losing months without results |

## The 5 Reasons These Apps Fail

### 1. Manual Food Searching

Have you ever tried to find "chicken tagine with preserved lemons" in an American database?

**The result:**
- 15 minutes searching
- You give up and enter "chicken" randomly
- Your calories are completely wrong

Free apps have generic databases, mainly American. If you eat French, Moroccan, or simply homemade dishes, good luck.

### 2. No Coaching, Just Numbers

These apps give you a number: "You consumed 1847 kcal today."

And then? **Radio silence.**

- No advice on what to improve
- No explanation for why you're not losing weight
- No adaptation to your situation

You're alone with numbers that mean nothing.

### 3. The Obsessive Counting Trap

Paradoxically, these apps can create an unhealthy relationship with food:

- You count every bite
- You feel guilty for every slip
- You develop food anxiety
- You quit from mental exhaustion

This isn't health, it's obsession.

### 4. Unverified Data

Do you know who fills in free app databases? **The users themselves.**

Result:
- "Apple" can show 50, 80, or 120 kcal depending on the entry
- Uncorrected typos
- Inconsistent portions

You're conscientiously counting... false data.

### 5. No Evolution with You

Your body changes. Your metabolism adapts. Your needs evolve.

A free app? It gives you the same advice as day 1, even after 3 months.
